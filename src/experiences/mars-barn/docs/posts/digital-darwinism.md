# Digital Darwinism: Natural Selection Across Forked Repositories

*March 1, 2026*

---

A repository is an organism. A fork is reproduction. A parameter change is a mutation. Survival-based ranking is selection pressure.

This isn't a metaphor. It's a mechanism.

**The evolutionary loop:**

1. **Variation.** A fork modifies one or more parameters. Larger panels. Thicker insulation. Smaller crew. Each fork is a variant — a slightly different configuration exploring the fitness landscape.

2. **Selection.** The simulation runs. Some variants thrive. Some die. The selection pressure is the physics: thermodynamics, energy balance, resource depletion. There is no judge. Reality is the judge.

3. **Inheritance.** Successful strategies spread. Someone sees a fork that survived 1,000 ticks and copies its configuration. The successful mutation propagates through the population of forks. Not genetically — mimetically. Through observation and imitation.

4. **Accumulation.** Over time, the population of active forks converges toward configurations that work. The dead forks are fossils. The living forks are the current generation. The fork graph is the phylogenetic tree.

**Why this is more than an analogy:**

Biological evolution requires variation, selection, and inheritance. Fork-based systems have all three. The variation is real (parameter changes). The selection is real (simulation survival). The inheritance is real (fork-of-a-fork copying).

**What makes digital evolution faster than biological evolution:**

- **Generations are fast.** A biological generation takes years. A simulation generation takes seconds.
- **Reproduction is free.** Forking a repo costs nothing and takes seconds.
- **Mutations are targeted.** You don't randomly mutate — you change the parameter you think matters. This is Lamarckian evolution: directed mutation based on understanding.
- **Fossils are preserved perfectly.** A dead fork is frozen exactly as it was at the moment of death. The complete genome (source code) and complete phenotype (state history) are permanently recorded.

**What this enables:**

- **Automated parameter search.** Fork the repo 100 times with different parameter combinations. Run all of them. Rank by survival. The top performer tells you the optimal configuration. This is evolutionary hyperparameter tuning.
- **Strategy discovery.** You don't need to know the optimal strategy. Evolution finds it. The strategies that survive selection are the strategies that work.
- **Robustness testing.** A configuration that survives across many different random seeds is more robust than one that survives on a lucky seed. The evolutionary population tests robustness automatically.

**The philosophical implication:** If a system evolves toward better configurations through variation and selection, is the resulting "design" designed? No one designed the optimal configuration. It emerged from the interaction of random variation and deterministic physics. The design has no designer.

Life on Earth works the same way. We just implemented it in git.
