Fastest path

Understand the whole application in ten minutes

1

Open the simulation

Open Viewer. Choose Classroom Quick Start for a pinned guided run, or choose Optimus Pathfinder to see robot operations and kited vTwin collaboration.

2

Move from orbit to the colony

Use the zoom controls to move through planet, tactical warmap, base, module, and crew views. The ground view is where crew, robots, modules, and kited vTwins become embodied actors.

3

Watch the kites

KITE-SCOUT and KITE-OPS are virtual twins, not crew. Their literal kites remain above them while they observe, interview, control, or collaborate.

4

Pair Mission Control

Open Mission Control in another tab. Compare the same six-digit PIN in both tabs. Click CONFIRM MATCH in Control and ALLOW in Viewer.

5

Influence one outcome

After pairing, inspect state or adjust allocations from Mission Control. In an Optimus or Hybrid mission, a stuck-fastener task can also offer DEPLOY KITED TWIN TEAM.

6

Read the evidence

Open the event feed and echo stream. They show what changed, which twin acted, what evidence it used, and whether an intervention made the run assisted and unranked.

The main experience

Choose humans, robots, hybrid crews, severe dust, broken ISRU, or the lunar testbed. Each mission changes the survival contract.

Orbit and warmap

Use orbit for the whole mission and the warmap for colony location, status, and tactical context.

See modules, crew, robots, weather, lighting, telepresence actors, targets, and task-related movement in one 3D scene.

Track resources, system efficiency, crew health, events, research, modules, LisPy policy, and the intervention ledger.

Embodied virtual twins

2. Follow the kite-marked vTwins

Kited vTwins are telepresence actors inside the simulation. They are deliberately separate from crew: they do not consume oxygen, water, food, earn crew health, or increase colony headcount.

KITE-SCOUT

Observes system state and interviews the exact crew member or robot involved in a problem. It creates structured evidence without applying the repair itself.

KITE-OPS

Consumes verified evidence, controls the approved operation, collaborates with Scout, and verifies that the task's existing success condition was applied once.

ModeColorWhat you see
OBSERVECyanKite, target tether, scan activity, and direct state evidence.
INTERVIEWVioletKite remains above Scout while a bounded question and answer are visible.
CONTROLAmberOps cites evidence and applies a capability-checked operation.
COLLABORATEGreenBoth twins share the target, evidence chain, and verification phase.

Try the shared repair

  1. Launch Optimus Pathfinder or Hybrid Colony.
  2. Run until a robot raises STUCK FASTENER - MANUAL OVERRIDE.
  3. Choose DEPLOY KITED TWIN TEAM.
  4. Advance four committed sols and watch the phase change above each kite.
Observe - 6 kWh-> Interview - 8 kWh-> Control - 18 kWh-> Verify - 6 kWh

The team pays 38 kWh, uses the existing task's repair effect, and records both participants, every phase, evidence IDs, the exact target robot, and the direct before/after delta.

Human intent + cryptographic authority

3. Pair a connection with the six-digit PIN

The PIN is the final human key. It confirms that a person intends to join these two exact tabs. Ephemeral P-256 keys authenticate both directions underneath it; the PIN is not used as the sole security mechanism.
1

Keep Viewer open

A mission can be running or paused. The Viewer is the authority that must approve the connection.

2

Open Mission Control in a second tab

Control generates an ephemeral P-256 key and requests a bounded session. It cannot read state or issue commands yet.

3

Compare all six digits

The same zero-padded code appears in both tabs. If the numbers differ or the requester is unfamiliar, choose DENY or CANCEL.

4

Confirm on both sides

Click CONFIRM MATCH in Mission Control and ALLOW in Viewer. Either order is supported, but both approvals are mandatory.

5

Look for TWIN LINKED

Only after dual confirmation and bidirectional signature verification do controls unlock and the external connection appear as a kite-marked, identity-unverified actor.

Pairing never survives a security boundary. Viewer reset, mission launch, cartridge import, either page reload, denial, expiry, or cancellation revokes authority. Pair again with a fresh PIN.
The paired operator surface

4. Use Mission Control

Once TWIN LINKED appears, each request is signed, strictly sequenced, capability-checked, and targeted to the paired session. Anonymous channel listeners receive no live colony, echo, wallet, cartridge, or attestation data.

Inspect

GET STATE, GET ECHO, GET INERTIA, GET SCORE, and ATTEST return signed Viewer data.

Control allocations

Adjust Heating, ISRU, Greenhouse, and rationing. Values are normalized and rejected atomically when invalid.

Run LisPy

Execute a bounded program against current colony state or push a new browser governor. Results return in signed canonical receipts.

Emergency stop

Stops the simulation clock through a signed control command. Pairing and intervention provenance remain visible.

(begin
  (set! heating_alloc 0.20)
  (set! isru_alloc 0.55)
  (set! greenhouse_alloc 0.25))
Assistance is honest. A paired external mutation enters the intervention ledger and makes the run assisted and unranked. Reads and rejected commands do not pretend to change the colony.
Decisions with consequences

5. Understand tasks and influence

Tasks emerge from echo data: falling resources, equipment conditions, crew health, weather, or a robot that is outside its confidence threshold. Choices mutate the same simulation state that the next sol consumes.

ChoiceMeaning
ApproveAccept the requested action and its explicit tradeoff.
DenyKeep the current policy or accept the denial consequence.
AlternativeChoose a compromise, EVA, split allocation, or other bounded route.
Override sequenceSend ordered commands with transit delay; wrong order or timeout can worsen damage.
Kited teamDelegate an eligible problem to complementary observe/interview/control capabilities.

Task history keeps the actor, source echo, before state, after state, direct delta, timeout status, and any kited evidence chain.

The colony nervous system

6. Read echo frames

Every committed sol creates an echo frame. Frame N describes what changed; Frame N+1 consumes that result. This is how weather, hazards, tasks, visuals, reflexes, and vTwin actions remain connected.

Delta

Resource and state movement for the whole sol. It is not mislabeled as one actor's direct effect.

Events

Dust, radiation, equipment, construction, research, crew, and terminal transitions.

Kited evidence

New observations, interviews, completed problem traces, participants, and direct task deltas.

Reflexes and CRI

Mid-heartbeat responses and the Colony Risk Index explain pressure building between frames.

Carry the organism

7. Save, export, import, and replay cartridges

A simulation cartridge carries colony state, echo memory, decisions, interventions, vTwin evidence, RNG checkpoint, score, and trust metadata.

  1. Use the cartridge controls in Viewer to export a run.
  2. Import or drop the cartridge into Viewer to resume supported state.
  3. Use Replay to inspect retained frames and decisions.
  4. Use SimHub to compare public results.
Live authority is never carried in a cartridge. PINs, private keys, open challenges, and connected sessions are runtime-only. Imported identities and evidence are historical and unverified until a fresh pairing occurs.
Large deterministic cohorts

8. Open the Mission Readiness Observatory

The Extinction Cascade Observatory compares 1,000 deterministic missions before and after the reliability corrections. It separates direct state evidence from temporal association.

Before

0% survived the 500-sol horizon; restricted mean survival was 42.14 sols.

Corrected model

23.5% survived; restricted mean survival reached 285.19 sols while retaining real failure risk.

Controls

No-hazard runs remain sustainable, proving the model is not simply delaying inevitable extinction.

This report is analysis. The interactive product remains Viewer, where decisions and kited actors are experienced in the world.

Words used throughout the app

10. Glossary and next links

vTwin

A virtual twin that observes or acts inside the same simulation protocol as physical and human participants.

Kited vTwin

An embodied vTwin with a literal kite above it, making identity, mode, target, and influence visible.

Echo frame

The bounded delta/evidence frame generated each sol and consumed by the next.

CRI

Colony Risk Index, a 0-100 summary of accumulating mission pressure.

LisPy

The small policy language used for browser-side governors, task automation, and economic rules.

Cartridge

A portable simulation checkpoint with state, evidence, decisions, and trust boundaries.
