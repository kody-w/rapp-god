The biggest improvements

Filter the story by the kind of improvement you care about.

GAME

The first tick actually works

The fatal research-event ordering bug was removed, WebGL became optional, and a dashboard fallback starts missions even on low-end or headless browsers.

Why cool: the ambitious demo became a game you can reliably start and play.

MISSIONS

Robots are finally robots

OPT and LUN crews no longer breathe or eat, but they do consume power and fail when power is gone. Lunar darkness, ISRU repair, Dust Bowl storms, resupply rules, and living victory are executable.

Why cool: mission selection changes the actual survival problem, not just the skin.

PHYSICS

Resources have real costs

ISRU and greenhouse output now debit process energy and water. Heating converts kW to full-sol kWh. Water recycling, solar, crops, and radiation research now affect measured simulation behavior.

Why cool: a winning strategy must obey a ledger instead of creating free matter.

FRAME LEDGER

1,067 frames reconciled safely

Every immutable frame is indexed with SHA-256 file identity. Publication is frozen against collisions, truncation, tampering, gaps, and unsupported schemas. Mixed legacy frames normalize at consumption.

Why cool: every player can face the same auditable Mars history.

SECURITY

Untrusted data stays untrusted

Cartridges, replay files, peer messages, SimHub fields, iframe content, twin commands, wallet transfers, and the local proxy gained validation, sandboxing, escaping, bounds, and fail-closed behavior.

Why cool: sharing a run no longer means trusting arbitrary executable content.

DETERMINISM

Save means resume

The browser PRNG cursor is serialized. Multi-colony governors and journals are stable across processes. Event duration, terminal ticks, chain blocks, and replay windows now have explicit deterministic rules.

Why cool: two copies of the same experiment can produce the same evidence.

RELEASE ENGINEERING

One command proves the build

npm test runs a dependency-free Python collector, frame-ledger validation, Node security/contract tests, and Playwright journeys. Read-only CI runs the same gate.

Why cool: the test command is now the executable product contract.

AUTONOMY

Assistance is recorded, not hidden

Human tasks, emergency actions, landing control, twin commands, and Earth supply launches enter an intervention ledger. Assisted and legacy runs cannot quietly claim autonomous status.

Why cool: the scoreboard can distinguish an AI achievement from a human rescue.

How the evolution unfolded

The work deliberately moved from “can it run?” to “can it be trusted?”

Cycles 1-4
Stabilize the organism. Browser ticks, WebGL fallback, robot metabolism, selected LisPy programs, frame reconciliation, atomic terminal states, and strict cartridge/twin validation.
Cycles 5-8
Make runs portable and accountable. Chain checks, intervention provenance, RNG checkpoints, truthful replay windows, mission victory, Dust Bowl behavior, mission parity, sabotage, and conserved trade.
Cycles 9-13
Close the physics and verification gaps. Dehydration, allocation simplex, governor memory, process power/water, event effects, attestation calldata, exact event lifetimes, research physics, and flare-dose parity.
Cycles 14+
Harden every boundary. Missing-frame blocking, replay safety, wallet invariants, Lunar/ISRU mission rules, physical-twin rollback protection, and cross-page telemetry fidelity.

The honest remaining frontier

One shared rules kernel

Several historical browser/Node/Python engines still encode overlapping simulation logic. Cross-runtime golden traces are the next architectural ceiling.

Exhaustive hazard dispatch

The public ledger contains more hazard families than every consumer currently executes. Official runs should eventually handle or explicitly classify every type.

One official scoring vocabulary

Game points, autonomy sols, diagnostic fitness, and official Monte Carlo scores must remain clearly versioned and never masquerade as real-world certification.
