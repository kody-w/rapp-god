🔴 FIRST PRINCIPLES TO MARS

An Oregon Trail for Mars. Real physics. Real weather. Real decisions.

What is this?

A Mars colony survival simulator that runs on real Mars weather data and real physics. You're mission control. An AI governor runs the colony autonomously. When it can't handle something, it escalates to you — approve resource reroutes, guide robots through physical repairs with light delay, decide who gets the water when there isn't enough.

Every session starts with today's actual Mars conditions. Play tomorrow and it's different.

How it works

Pick a mission → configure crew (humans, robots, or both) → AI + LisPy automation runs the colony sol by sol → Papers Please-style tasks arrive when things go wrong → your decisions have cascading consequences → colony lives or dies → post-mortem traces exactly what killed it → try again.

The Nervous System

The colony doesn't just tick once per sol and go blank. It has a nervous system — echo frames capture every heartbeat, inertia signals track trajectories (not just thresholds), and 10 reflex arcs fire between heartbeats to keep the colony alive. When O₂ is trending down, the colony auto-boosts ISRU before you even see the warning.

Key Features

🌡️ Real Mars Weather

NASA data models. Current temperature, dust, solar, wind, season. Seeds every session.

🤖 Manual Override

Guide robots through physical tasks with light delay. Stuck bolt? Solar panel cleaning? Airlock seal? You send the commands.

📊 Colony Risk Index

A VIX for Mars. 0-100. Computed by LisPy every sol. Higher CRI = more things go wrong. Risk accumulates.

💀 Post-Mortem

Every death traces the full causal chain. Decision history, unintended consequences, what might have saved you.

🧠 LisPy VM

Write your own governor AI in sandboxed Lisp. The same code runs in the browser and on the Python sim.

🌐 3D Digital Twin

Globe.gl planet → Three.js ground view. Crew/robots match your config. Lights go dark when rationing.
