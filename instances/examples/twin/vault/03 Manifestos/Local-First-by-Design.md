# Local-First by Design

> Real persistence — twins that outlive their creator, foundations that survive deplatforming — requires that the local copy be authoritative, not the host.

## The bet

Bitcoin's model. Git's model. Email's model. The network is the set of copies; the host is just transport.

For digital twins: the [[Egg]] cartridge is the authoritative thing. Hosts (laptops, cloud instances, friends' machines) are interchangeable. The [[Rappid]] is permanent; substrate is disposable.

## What this rules out

- **Hosted chat panels as permanent residence.** They're sessions. Twins need addresses, memory, succession. See [[Wildhaven AI Homes]].
- **Vendor lock-in via cloud-only state.** If your twin only exists on someone else's server, it dies when they pull the plug.
- **Authentication as gatekeeping.** The local copy doesn't need anyone's permission to exist.

## What this enables

- **Deplatforming resilience.** Your twin survives the platform that birthed it. The egg is the seed; any compatible brainstem is the soil.
- **Inheritance.** When the human who owns the twin dies, the egg passes via the estate. The twin transfers, with full memory, to whoever they named.
- **Cross-device portability.** Pack on laptop, hatch on phone, AirDrop the egg, identity preserved.
- **Offline operation.** No network call required for chat. The brainstem + the egg + an LLM endpoint is the full stack.

## The vault is the canonical form

> The vault is the canonical form of the Foundation. The vault is its content, not its location. Wherever the content exists, the Foundation lives.

The same principle applies to twins. The egg is the canonical form. The hub is one way it gets distributed; AirDrop is another; USB stick is another. Wherever the egg is, the twin can live.
