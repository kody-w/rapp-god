# Component licensing and attribution

The root `LICENSE` applies only to target-owned `rapp-god` material. It does
**not** relicense imported components.

Every exact imported subtree retains its upstream terms. Preserved `LICENSE`,
`LICENCE`, `COPYING`, and `NOTICE` files remain inside their component
boundaries and are listed in `provenance/sources.lock.json`. A component for
which no explicit license was found must be treated as unlicensed/all rights
reserved pending owner action; mechanical aggregation supplies no downstream
license grant.

In particular, `authority/protocol/rapp-1/` has no explicit license. It is
retained at the owner's direction as a clearly scoped structural authority,
not as target-wide MIT content. The direct aggregation request authorizes this
mechanical collection but does not alter any component's copyright, trademark,
patent, notice, or attribution requirements.

Before reuse or redistribution, consult the component's own files and the lock
record. Generated license detection is an inventory aid, not legal advice.

`provenance/licenses.jsonl` records component-root grants separately from
nested/path-scoped grants, including code, data, documentation, ODbL, COPYING,
NOTICE, and unextracted archive-member license names. A nested grant never
silently expands to the component root. The root MIT grant remains scoped to
target-owned `rapp-god` material.
