# Council: Sunset Clauses for Failed Pillars

## Positions on the table

**Builder:** Sunset clauses force focus and protect against sunk-cost work.
**Gardener:** Sunset clauses kill compounding experiments. Prune branches, don't chop trees.
**Operator:** Tired. Just tell me what to do tomorrow.

## Additional voices

**Archivist:** A sunset isn't a deletion — it's a *demotion*. Move the Pillar to `dormant/` with a one-paragraph postmortem. The work compounds even when the Pillar doesn't.

**Auditor:** Without a clock, every Pillar becomes load-bearing by default. Six months in, the operator can't tell live ambition from abandoned scaffolding. That's how organisms get cancer.

**Forager:** The question isn't *whether* to sunset, it's *what triggers it*. Time-based sunsets are dumb. Signal-based sunsets — "no commits in 30 days AND no stated intent to resume" — survive both Builder and Gardener objections.

## Synthesis

Adopt **dormancy, not deletion**, with **signal-based triggers, not calendar-based ones**.

Concrete proposal:
1. Each Pillar carries a `last_signal` timestamp (any commit, note, or referenced execution).
2. After **60 days of silence**, Continuum auto-files a *Dormancy Review*: a one-page markdown summarizing what was tried, what was learned, what blocked it.
3. The Operator does nothing. If they want to revive, they touch the Pillar — that's the signal. If they don't, the Pillar moves to `pillars/dormant/` after another 30 days. Files preserved. Lessons compound.
4. **No Pillar is ever deleted.** Gardener wins on substrate. Builder wins on attention. Operator wins on default-do-nothing.

For the tired Operator tomorrow: **don't decide anything about sunsets**. Just commit the dormancy-review template to `templates/dormancy-review.md` so the mechanism exists when you have energy to wire it up. Future-you will thank present-you for shipping the *option* instead of the *policy*.
