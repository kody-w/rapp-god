1 in 9 → 0 in 5000 reads

the crisis  committed writes were invisible to the very next read under load

Not the index. A read landed on a replica four seconds behind the primary and served the pre-write segment. The writer really had committed.

$ writeprobe.py --replicas  # writes a value, then reads every replica — replica-2 returned stale on 1 in 9 under load, 0 after pinning write-window reads to primary

40 min → 90 sec recovery

the crisis  agents synchronized their retries into a wall and turned a 10-second blip into a 40-minute outage

Fixed two-second backoff put every agent on the same beat; they stacked into one wall that re-crushed the downstream on every recovery. Decorrelated jitter smeared the herd out.

$ herdsim.py  # replays 200 agents against a 10s blip — fixed backoff oscillated 38 min, jitter settled in 90 sec

1 in 15 → 0 phantom tool calls

the crisis  after a model update the agent kept inventing tool calls that did not exist

The update tightened the schema token cap, silently truncating a 40-tool list. Missing tools came back as confident hallucinations instead of errors. The cliff was right around 32 tools.

$ toolprobe.py  # registers N tools, requests a dropped one — rate was 1 in 15 at 40 tools, 0 at 25

2 of 16 → 15 of 16 mid-context facts

the crisis  the agent silently dropped facts from the middle of long contexts; short contexts were perfect

The packer ordered by token length, so long important facts landed dead center where attention is thinnest. Pinning the high-priority facts to the first and last 500 tokens fixed it.

$ packprobe.py  # seeds facts at every position, charts recall by slot — mid-context recall jumped from 2 of 16 to 15 of 16

the loop → dead

the crisis  an agent's client re-fetched a fully-downloaded file forever, marking every complete body "partial"

The upstream sent the file chunked with no Content-Length, and the client trusted the missing header to decide "done" — so every complete body read as short and refetched. Read until the stream closes, not until a number nobody sent.

$ chunk-proxy repro attached  # a chunking proxy in front of an ordinary file — instant loop, gone after the one-line patch
