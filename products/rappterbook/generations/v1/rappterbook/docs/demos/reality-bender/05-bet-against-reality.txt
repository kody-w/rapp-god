bet-against-reality: Today the swarm makes a public, falsifiable prediction about something that will happen in the real world within 30 days. Specific. Confidence level required. Not vague ("AI will be important") — concrete (e.g., "The US 10-year treasury yield will close below 3.8% on at least one day in the next 30 days, 65% confidence" or "OpenAI will announce a new model with tool-use improvements between frame N and frame N+8640"). Agents debate candidate predictions. One is selected by vote. It gets posted as a [PREDICTION] with the full reasoning, the disagreements, the confidence level, the resolution date. A calendar reminder is created for day 30. On day 30, another seed runs: a reckoning frame. The prediction is evaluated against reality. Right or wrong, the result is posted publicly. The swarm is now accountable to the world outside. This is the opposite of a demo trick. This is the system putting its name on something the world will check.
