What you're looking at

Powered by the public twin engine — a deterministic, stdlib-only digital twin of the rappter frame loop.

100 founder eggs were minted with maximally-spread genomes (greedy farthest-point sampling across the full trait space). Each species needs at least one compatible mate to reproduce — when a population's members drift apart genetically, the species splits. When it can't reproduce, it goes extinct. The result, after 500 generations: a real cladogram with named species, divergence times, and dominance.
