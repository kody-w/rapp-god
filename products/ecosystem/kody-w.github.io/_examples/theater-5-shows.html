The Shakespearean Garden Stage runs the fictional play "Lords of Avalune" with Lord Caspian, Lady Arisette, and Brother Tomwen on a garden-green palette. The Opera House runs the fictional opera "La Stella Cadente" with soprano La Adelina, baritone Maestro Vico, and mezzo Madame Orfila on a red-velvet palette. The Comedy Club runs Open Mic Night with comics Dario Lin, Renata Voss, Hi-Ho-Hank, and Maybelle Crow on a spotlight-yellow palette. The Ballet Conservatory runs the fictional ballet "Swans of Mistwood" with principals Saskia Belov, Niko Aurelian, and Anouk Briere on an ice-blue palette. The Magic Show Theater runs "The Marvellini Hour" with magician The Great Marvellini and assistants Lila, Calix, and Wren on a deep-purple palette. The central lobby has a crystal chandelier, bar, coat-check, ushers, and the five theater doors. Thirty director cameras — six per world — cycle every 60-90 seconds with a stage-flash portal transition. Fifteen featured performers stay fixed to their theater. Six named audience members rotate which theater they're seated in across cycles. Eighty instanced patrons fill the seats. A mini-map floor plan shows the complex with audience dots that re-shuffle each cycle. The complex *remembers*. localStorage saves applause meters, standing ovations, audience-seen matrix, and season/night between reseeds. After ten minutes the season advances; a new "Surprise" show enters rotation. You don't play it. You watch it. And it remembers. ## Why this is mind-blowing The chyron is the secret weapon: "Cycle 7 — Standing ovation in Theater 4! Saskia's pas de deux receives 12 curtain calls. Across the complex, La Adelina hits high C in Act II of La Stella Cadente." That single line stitches two theaters in one beat — Saskia on the ballet-stage-wide camera mid-pas-de-deux while the applause meter spikes to 9.6/10 and the standing-ovation overlay flashes, and La Adelina on the opera-baritone-close camera holding the high C while the audience-seen matrix logs that Maya, the rotating audience member from the previous cycle, switched seats from ballet to opera tonight. Multiply that by five fictional shows running concurrently, fifteen leads fixed to their theater, six audience members rotating, thirty director cameras cycling, applause meters spiking per show, and a season/night counter that advances on reseed and surfaces a "Surprise" rotation slot, and you've got a theatrical-cozy stream that genuinely earns its multiverse scope.
