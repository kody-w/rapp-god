The behavior sells the time of day." - "A 6-hero rotating roster turns one engine into 6 different watching experiences — the streamer never sees the same hero twice in a row." --- ## What this is A **single-file autonomous 3D RPG** that picks a hero, drops them in a fantasy continent, and lets them play the entire campaign on autoplay. Seven hand-built zones, 10 quests, a dragon boss, a chronicle scroll, then the next hero in the roster takes over. Forever. ## Why this is mind-blowing The hero walks out of Whitewatch village at dawn — torches still glowing, villagers tending market stalls, well in the square. They cross Daggerwood (140 trees, a bandit camp with a fire), climb Frostfang Pass over a rope bridge, descend into Bleakhall to loot a sarcophagus in a ruined keep, ferry across to Ashford where a watermill turns and rowboats bob at the dock, kneel at the Standing Stones to gain a buff, then ascend Skyreach for the dragon battle. The dragon has six body segments that ripple, wings that flap, a fire-breath cone that the hero dodges. After the kill: a 9.5-second scrolling chronicle screen narrates what just happened. Then the next hero — Sigrid the Wise, or Arn Coldforge, or Eira Wolfmother — boots up. The whole 90-second day/night cycle runs underneath: sun rotates, sky lerps dawn→day→dusk→night, 900 stars fade in, torches light up, villagers retreat indoors when night falls. **It's autonomous Skyrim in 65 KB.**
