Twelve procedural bands run a staggered schedule. A 260-strong crowd with five genre-driven behaviors flows between stages as sets start. The sky lerps from afternoon to sunset to full night and a director cuts between eight cinematic cameras as the headliner takes Main East. ## Why this is mind-blowing The schedule grid is real — bands actually start and finish on it. The crowd actually migrates. The drop flash actually fires on the headliner's drop. The fireworks finale actually closes the festival. Then the world reseeds with twelve new bands across the four stages and starts the day all over again. Watching it is like camping at a festival without the sunburn.
