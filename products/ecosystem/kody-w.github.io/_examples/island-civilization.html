Twelve founders led by Lani, Kapono, Mahina, Ikaika, Nalu, Pohai, Manu, and Hina arrive on the founding beach in Year 1 with a single outrigger canoe. By Year 12 there are three huts. By Year 35 the taro terraces are stepped up the volcano slopes. By Year 80 the longhouse stands with smoke rising from the meeting circle and Makoa — Lani's great-grandson — is crowned First Chief. Standing stones are raised at Years 100, 120, 140, 160, and 180. By Year 180 a sun-dial pillar marks the ceremonial circle and a 30-canoe fishing fleet works the lagoon. Eight named sub-locations are live in the same scene: Founding Beach, Taro Terraces, Chief's Hall, Fishing Lagoon, Ceremonial Circle, Forest Interior, Sacred Mountain Peak, Coastal Village. Twenty director cameras cut between beach landing, canoe carving, taro terraces, longhouse fire, ceremonial dawn, mountain peak, lagoon under-swim, chief's chair, drum circle, sunset palms, forest interior, fishing fleet, village overlook, standing stones night, palm grove, coastal drift, aerial, canoe wake. Procedural volcanic terrain is vertex-colored by elevation. The lagoon is a translucent shader. Forty-eight palms sway. Smoke rises from the longhouse fire pit. The island *remembers*. localStorage saves eight named ancestors with role + descendants + songs between reseeds. After ten minutes the lineage extends — a new generation appears. You don't play it. You watch it. And it remembers. ## Why this is mind-blowing The chyron is the secret weapon: "Year 47 — Lani's grandson Keoni catches first marlin at twelve years old. Manu's lineage carves new canoe." That single line stitches together founder Lani (Year 1 beach landing camera), descendant Keoni (Gen 2, born Year 3, currently a child on the lagoon-fishing camera), and founder Manu (Year 1 founder, now elder under the palms watching his descendants carve a new outrigger on the canoe-carving camera). All three are visible *simultaneously* across three director cameras while the chyron prints.
