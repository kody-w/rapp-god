Five named teachers and twelve named kids visibly live a school day across five areas. Classroom 3B is the warm-pencil-yellow homeroom with whiteboard, terrarium (Mr. Greenly the school turtle lives there), and easel. The Cafeteria is the bright cafeteria-tray-orange room with the milk-carton fortress under construction (Tommy's project). The Playground is the noon-cyan swings + slide + hopscotch grid + sandbox (Aisha's hopscotch championship is in session). The Gymnasium is the shiny-wood-amber court with hoops, parachute, and dodgeball setup (Mr. Chen's domain). The Library is the soft-rainbow beanbags + story circle + rainbow rug (Ms. Reyes's quiet kingdom). A school bell rings every sixty to ninety seconds and triggers the area transition — the camera follows the kids as they file out of one area and into the next. Ms. Lopez (homeroom 3B), Mr. Chen (gym), Ms. Brigham (cafeteria), Ms. Reyes (library), Principal Underwood (cozy administrator who knows every kid's name). Tommy (milk-carton fortress architect — south wall is the 12-cube challenge), Aisha (hopscotch championship — undefeated cycle 4), Diego, Lily, Wesley, Chloe, Marcus, Priya, Emmett, Sara, Toby, Sophie. Each teacher and kid has a goal, a need, a plan, a current step, a mood, and a teacher note — all visible in the AGENTIC THINK PANEL. Tommy's plan reads "collect 12 milk cartons → build south wall → defend snack break → diplomacy with Aisha for east annex." Plus 80 instanced ambient kids filling the world. Nineteen director cameras cycle. Sticker chart top-right shows areas-visited per kid. Goal trails on the floor. Decision diamonds pop on plan changes. Sparkle bursts fire on friendships. The school *remembers*. localStorage saves the day counter, total stickers earned, total books read in library, total hopscotch champs crowned, total cafeteria trades, total nap mat naps, and a friendships graph (Tommy + Diego paired up 7 cycles, Aisha + Lily 9 cycles). Each ten-minute reseed advances the day, totals persist, and a fresh schedule rolls. You don't play it. You watch it. And it remembers. ## Why this is mind-blowing The chyron is the secret weapon: "Day 14 — Tommy's south wall hits 12 milk cartons. Aisha defends hopscotch on cycle 4. Marcus reads 'The Ladybug Detective' in library." That single line stitches three areas in one beat — Tommy on the Cafeteria milk-fortress close camera with the 12th carton clicking into place (decision diamond as Tommy realizes he can defend snack break, plan-step ticks 3 of 4), Aisha on the Playground hopscotch close camera with her sneaker landing perfectly on square 7 (interaction sparkle as Lily acknowledges the championship, hopscotch champ counter ticks +1), and Marcus on the Library beanbag close camera with the (fictional) "Ladybug Detective" cover catching the soft-rainbow light (decision diamond as Marcus turns to chapter 5, books-read counter ticks +1). Multiply that by twelve kids each with a brain panel showing kid-scale plans (milk-carton diplomacy, hopscotch championships, library reading marathons), five teachers including a cozy principal who knows every name, a school bell that triggers area transitions every sixty to ninety seconds, sticker chart compounding across the school year, friendships graph compounding across reseeds, and a Recess-cozy chyron beating Day×Bell, and you've got a wholesome stream that genuinely earns its agentic scope.
