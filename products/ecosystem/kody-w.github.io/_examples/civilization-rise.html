Deterministic endings kill the genre." --- ## What this is A **single-file autonomous 3D Civilization-style 4-age saga**. 4 civs colonize an 8×8 hex world, build wonders, research the tech tree from Pottery to Singularity, fight wars, suffer plagues, and either ascend to Type-II Kardashev or collapse into a new dark age. 8 minutes per saga. Forever. ## Why this is mind-blowing **AGE 1 — STONE & CLASSICAL.** Sky is muted ochre. Hexes are pristine. Four civs of huts. Prof. Helena Vance (white anchor PIP, scholarly): *"This is CIV-NET. Welcome to the dawn of humankind."* Banner: *"AGE I — STONE & CLASSICAL — YEAR 0 OF HUMANKIND."* First city founded — banner: *"BLUE FOUNDS THEIR CAPITAL ON THE RIVERBANK."* Tech panel lights up: *"TECHNOLOGY UNLOCKED: POTTERY."* **AGE 3 — INDUSTRIAL.** Sky is sooty grey. Pollution tint over the hexes. Cities have skyscrapers. Doc Marsh (gold, futurist): *"The Industrial Revolution has transformed the planet — and the air with it."* Banner: *"WONDER COMPLETE: HOOVER DAM."* Tech tree: *"INDUSTRIALIZATION → COMPUTING UNLOCKED."* Then red banner: *"WORLD WAR DECLARED — RED vs PURPLE."* Cities pause growth. Battlefield cam swings to the front line. **AGE 4 — SPACE.** Sky clears. Domes rise. Spaceport built — rocket fires. *"MOON LANDING."* Then *"MARS COLONY."* Banner blooms gold: *"CIVILIZATION COMPLETE — ASCENSION TO TYPE-II KARDASHEV."* OR random 50/50: *"CIVILIZATION COLLAPSED — A NEW DARK AGE BEGINS."* 12-second civ-flag ceremony. Reseed. Year 0. Loop.
