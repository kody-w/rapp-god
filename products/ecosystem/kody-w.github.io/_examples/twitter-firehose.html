Tone: feels like watching a real platform's live ops dashboard, not a demo. lessons: - "Procedural cities + 3D-to-screen projection is the cheapest way to make a globe feel populated." - "Inverting engagement ratios is more dramatic than just spiking one number — the cancel event proves it." - "A trending sidebar that reorders is as much story as the feed itself; viewers track the leaderboard." --- ## What this is A single-file three.js demo that turns a 3D rotating globe into a live social-network operations room. Sixty procedural users post into a timeline, trending tags shuffle as topics heat up, and every tweet pulses a city pin you can see from orbit. Then the world events fire — a viral chain, a ratio, a cancel storm, a subtweet war, breaking news — and the director cuts between seven cinematic cameras as the chaos unfolds. ## Why this is mind-blowing It feels like a live ops console, not a demo. The combination of a real 3D globe, projected DOM labels, a reordering trending leaderboard, and engagement counters that climb in real time tricks your brain into watching a platform instead of a script. Every 8–10 minutes the world reseeds with a flash and a brand-new cast of 60 users, brand-new trending topics, and a brand-new dramatic arc — so it never loops the same story twice.
