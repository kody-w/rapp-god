What this is

One hundred named bots drop into a procedural map with eight points of interest — city, residential, military, forest, outpost, farms, docks, bunker — plus lakes and bridges. A purple storm wall closes in over six phases. Loot comes in four tiers (white, green, blue, purple). One or two airdrops mid-match deliver a legendary weapon to a marked location. The killfeed scrolls right, the kill leader's name pulses gold top-right, the alive count ticks down. When one bot is left, the screen explodes with confetti and the words VICTORY ROYALE in giant gold letters.

Why this is mind-blowing

Test runs produce real broadcast moments: "STORM kills DemonX (2 left) → InquisitorElite wins" with 20 kills as the kill leader. The drama-cam catches every fight that matters. The match length sits in the 3-5 minute range — perfect for a Twitch loop. Match-to-match the winner could be a city camper, a military rusher, or a forest sniper. There's no script — the same map seed produces wildly different stories.
