Fifty inmates, four gangs, three featured characters per day, and a director that cuts between eight cinematic cameras as the yard moves through count, workout, ball, payphones, visitation, and the slow gravity of time. ## Why this is mind-blowing Most prison sims chase exploitation. This one is the opposite. The narrator quotes are italic Herzog-toned reflections, the anchor PIP names a real correspondent, the lower-thirds tell you who someone is and why their daughter graduated yesterday. When a fight squares up the camera cuts away to the lockdown red flash and the count countdown. It feels like watching a real documentary, with a brand new cast of 50 every nine minutes.
