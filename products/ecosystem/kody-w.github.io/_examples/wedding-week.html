Seven sub-locations sit on one map: a chapel with an altar to the north, a reception tent to the south, a row of five cottages to the east, the getting-ready suites, a lakeside fireworks pier, a rehearsal terrace, and the drop-off gate. Sixty named cast — bride Lila Mendoza, groom Theo Pak, full Mendoza and Pak families, Rev. Patrice Allard, planner Quinn Rashid, DJ KAYV, Uncle Marv as the chyron heckler, bridesmaids, groomsmen, grandparents, neighbors, photographer, ex-boyfriend Drew, dog Rover, Baby Olive — share the estate with sixty InstancedMesh background extras. Six acts run on a roughly nine-minute loop: Rehearsal Dinner → Getting Ready → Ceremony → Dinner & Toasts → Reception & Fireworks → Farewell Brunch. Eighteen director cameras cut between altar, tent, cottage row, fireworks pier, terrace, and drop-off gate, weighted to the act in progress. You don't play it. You watch it. ## Why this is mind-blowing Each act has its own visual signature. Rehearsal Dinner lights up the terrace at dusk. Getting Ready cuts to the cottage row with the bridesmaids and groomsmen prepping in their own suites. Ceremony drops falling-petal particles at the altar and a vows quote panel cycles ten lines while Rev. Allard officiates. Dinner & Toasts pulls the cameras into the reception tent with 240+ string lights pulsing on the DJ speakers. Reception & Fireworks fires real fireworks bursts over the lake while DJ KAYV's beat lights up the speakers. Farewell Brunch lights a sparkler tunnel and the getaway car drives off as the chyron prints "AND THEY LIVED." Then the world reseeds with a new couple — Holt × Reyes, Okafor × Kaminski, or three other variants — and starts the week over with a fresh family roster. Uncle Marv runs comic relief in the chyron the whole time. It's the wedding video you'd actually leave on while you fold laundry.
