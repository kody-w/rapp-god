Floor 1 is the food court with warm-amber lighting and six fictional kiosks: pizza, sushi, ice-cream, coffee, smoothie, and pretzel, around a seating area with condiment-bar and return-trays. Floor 2 is fashion with runway-magenta lighting and three named boutiques: AVALUNE, KITSUNE STREET, METRO LANE, plus dressing rooms, shoe try-on bench, and a window display. Floor 3 is tech with cool-cyan lighting and a gaming store, electronics, wireless-carrier, repair kiosk, and a vintage-computer museum. Floor 4 is art with gallery-white lighting and three named galleries: AURELIO MODERN, CHROMATIC, INK-AND-WATER, plus a sculpture nook, bench, and gift shop. Floor 5 is the rooftop arcade with sunset-orange-purple lighting featuring mini-golf, batting cages, a retro arcade, a neon-signed rooftop bar, and a sunset deck. Twelve named shoppers visit multiple floors: Lou Park with kids Cassie and Reggie, teen pair Marisol and Tee, couple Cris and Dre, influencer Ren, solo gamer Vidal, mom Esi with toddler Ade, senior Mr. Whitford (a fun NEXLOGIC cameo from `megacorp-30yr`), and janitor Olaf. Twenty-one director cameras cycle: atrium fountain wide, glass elevator POV up and down, escalator side, food court wide, pizza counter close, fashion runway, dressing room reveal, tech gaming-store, vintage-computer close, art gallery wall, sculpture, rooftop mini-golf, batting cage, neon-bar, sunset deck, Olaf mop-cam, and drone-over-mall. The mall *remembers*. localStorage saves which floors each shopper visited, bags purchased, dollars spent, and a day counter between reseeds. After ten minutes the next mall day begins; returning shoppers arrive with new shopping lists. You don't play it. You watch it. And it remembers. ## Why this is mind-blowing The chyron is the secret weapon: "Cycle 5 — Lou Park buys 3 t-shirts at AVALUNE on Floor 2. Across the mall, Vidal beats his high score at Floor 5 arcade. Mr. Whitford coffees at Floor 1 before heading to NEXLOGIC." That single line stitches three floors in one beat — Lou on the fashion-runway camera at AVALUNE with three new shopping bags, Vidal on the rooftop-arcade camera celebrating his high score, and Mr. Whitford on the food-court coffee-counter close-up sipping before he leaves for NEXLOGIC, the same character from `megacorp-30yr` making a cross-demo cameo. Multiply that by 12 named shoppers, 100 instanced shoppers, 5 floors with their own palette and themed font, a glass-elevator-POV transition that actually feels like riding the elevator, and a localStorage memory that persists each shopper's bag count and dollar-spent across reseeds, and you've got a nostalgic-cozy stream that genuinely earns the comparison to a 1980s mall on a Saturday afternoon.
