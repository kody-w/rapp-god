In 1957 the deck has rope rigging, sextants, brass instruments, and sepia-gold light. In 2024 the deck has cyan buoys, GPS, spectrometers, and bright daylight. In 2089 the deck has a violet submersible, holographic readouts, and a softer violet-blue sea. All three are visible simultaneously through the cycle. Five ship sub-worlds: Bridge (helm + nav charts), Main Deck (where the era gear morphs), Galley (warm-amber wood tables that don't change much because cozy is timeless), Lab Bay (microscope vs spectrometer vs holo-array), Crew Quarters (bunks + sea-chest). 21 named crew members = 7 roles × 3 eras: Captain (Whitford-1957 / Brennan-2024 / Vega-2089), Lead Scientist (the Carrow LINEAGE — Wendell Carrow in 1957 sketches the creature in his journal, his daughter Esme Carrow in 2024 photographs it from a buoy, his great-grandkid Sora-Quist Carrow in 2089 holographs it from the submersible — same family, three eras, same creature), Bosun, Cook (recurring), Chief Engineer, Diver/Submersible Pilot, Radio/Comms Officer. Plus Bytes the cat in all three eras (because cats are timeless). Plus Pip across eras (Hatcher in 1957, Halloran-off-duty in 2024, Pip-7 in 2089 — same lineage, three roles). 80 instanced ambient + 30 name pool = 130 named souls. Nineteen director cameras include helm POV, deck-stroll, submersible-pilot, galley-warmth, lab-bench, crew-bunks, and splitscreen 3-era. The Timeline Fork Panel is a live 3-era badge UI (1957/2024/2089) that pulses with the canon era.
