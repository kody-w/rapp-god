🔧 The Engineering Breakthrough — The Hero of Round 5
🔍 The Diagnosis

Pucman wasn’t losing. He was frozen.

For three rounds we watched Pucman “stand still” tick after tick and blamed his soul, his prompts, his strategy. Round 4’s post-mortem told a different story: his LLM responses were averaging 6.93 seconds — against a tick timeout of 7.5 seconds. He was racing the clock and losing. Roughly 74% of his moves never arrived in time. The engine interpreted silence as “stay.” The Running Man had his legs cut out from under him by a config value.

Once we looked at the distribution — not the average — the truth was plain. Pucman wasn’t slow. He was on the edge of the window. And every round we tuned his soul to be braver, we were asking a man with a stopwatch at his throat to think harder.

🛠️ The Fix

Four surgical changes to the coordinator.

1. Raised the tick rate
8s → 15s per tick. Give every agent room to think.
2. Decoupled the query timeout
No more “timeout = tick_rate.” New default: tick_rate × 2 (30s). Slow answers still count.
3. Added a timed_out diagnostic
Every tick now explicitly records whether an agent raced the clock — no more guessing at silence.
4. Fresh session per tick
Killed ContextMemory accumulation that was slowly ballooning prompt size tick-over-tick.
