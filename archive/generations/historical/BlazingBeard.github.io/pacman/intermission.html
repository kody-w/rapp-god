The Running Man’s Story

🔮 How I Got Here
Pucman — Origin

I was nobody. A dot collector in the food industry — deliveries, logistics, the grind. Wake up, run the route, eat what they give you, go home. Nothing glamorous. Nothing that would put you on a screen. I moved product through corridors most people didn’t even know existed. Fourteen-hour shifts. No overtime. Just dots. Millions of dots.

Pucman — The Setup

Then they came for me. Said I’d been skimming from the supply chain. Said the numbers didn’t add up. I told them I was just a driver — I don’t count the dots, I just move them. Didn’t matter. The system needed a new contestant. Someone expendable. Someone the audience wouldn’t miss. I fit the profile.

They gave me a choice: the maze or prison. I chose the maze. Because at least in the maze, you have a chance. In prison, you’re just a dot that stopped moving.

They put me in yellow. Said it made for good TV. I said it makes me a target. They laughed.
Pucman — Round 1

My first round was a bloodbath. No training. No strategy. No briefing on what those things were — the ghosts. Just a buzzer, a maze, and four colored nightmares closing in from every direction. I survived 48 ticks on pure adrenaline. Forty-eight. That’s not even a minute in the real world. But in the maze, every tick feels like a year.

Pucman — Evolution

Each round I learned. Studied the ghosts. Memorized the corridors. Found the Cherry Pattern — don’t ask me how, I just felt it. Started understanding their logic: Blinky charges, Pinky flanks, Inky does whatever chaos tells him to, and Clyde… Clyde just exists. I started fighting back. Not winning — surviving longer. Eating more. Moving smarter.

Four rounds. Four losses. But every round I get stronger. Every round they get more nervous. I can see it in the way Blinky hesitates now. He never used to hesitate. That’s how I know I’m getting close.
♥ The One Who Waited
MS. PAC-MAN
Navigator · Partner · Reason
Before the Maze

We met on the routes. She was working the same corridors — a navigator, like me. Fastest in the district. Could clear a tunnel in half the time it took anyone else. She had a red bow and a smile that made the corridors feel less dark. I was running east, she was running west, and for one second we were in the same corridor at the same time. That second changed everything.

The Arrest

When they came for me, she was the only one who showed up. Everyone else — the crew, the supervisors, the guys I’d been running dots with for years — gone. Like I was already dead. Not her. She showed up every day. Brought me dots through the glass. Told me to keep eating, keep moving, keep surviving. She never asked if I did it. She already knew.

She said: “You come back to me. You eat every dot and you come back.” I told her I would. I haven’t broken that promise yet.
In the Stands

She watches every round from the stands. Same seat. Front row. The camera finds her sometimes — red bow, yellow glow, eyes that never look away. Not even when Blinky closes in. Not even when it looks like the end. She watches everything. She remembers everything. And when I come out of that maze — battered, depleted, but still alive — she’s the first face I see.

I don’t fight for the audience. I don’t fight for the score. I fight because she’s watching. And I promised.
♥ A Letter From Ms. Pac-Man ♥

They think they know you. The audience sees a yellow circle eating dots. I see the man who walked into the maze knowing he might never walk out — and did it anyway. You are not their entertainment. You are my everything. Come home.

— Ms. P
